# GTA and Star Citizen Format Direction

This document records how NEXUS Worlds can use the format strengths of GTA-style open-world games and Star Citizen-style sci-fi scale while remaining fully original.

## Legal Rule

Use these games only as broad format inspiration.

Do not copy:

- names
- maps
- characters
- vehicles or ships
- UI layouts exactly
- missions
- storylines
- gangs/factions
- brands
- radio/music
- city layouts
- protected assets

NEXUS Worlds must remain original.

## Core Blend

NEXUS Worlds should combine:

```text
GTA-style open-world freedom
+
Star Citizen-style sci-fi scale and travel fantasy
+
AI civilisation simulation
+
Founder-created personal story
```

The unique selling point is not crime or space ships alone.

The unique selling point is:

```text
A living AI world where citizens think, remember, work, build, travel, form society and evolve technology around the player.
```

## GTA-Style Format Principles

Use as inspiration:

- open-world freedom
- first-person/third-person exploration options later
- vehicles and movement freedom
- mission markers
- map icons
- side activities
- career paths
- world events
- characters with strong personalities
- city/settlement districts
- shops/services later
- player reputation
- emergent chaos and consequences

For NEXUS Worlds this becomes:

- first-person Founder exploration
- open settlement/city/world zones
- AI-driven missions
- career-based opportunities
- transport upgrades
- evolving settlement districts
- world events like storms, shortages, discoveries and disputes
- reputation with agents and society

## Star Citizen-Style Format Principles

Use as inspiration:

- sci-fi immersion
- planetary landing fantasy
- spaceport progression
- ships/vehicles later
- careers/roles
- future economy
- travel between worlds
- cinematic loading/launch sequences
- cockpit/first-person immersion
- world hubs
- large-scale future ambition

For NEXUS Worlds this becomes:

- Origin Planet start
- future spaceport unlock
- portal/world travel
- flying vehicles
- Founder careers
- settlement-to-city-to-space progression
- sci-fi HUD
- loading sequence into worlds
- future interplanetary civilisation

## NEXUS Worlds Original Format

The player starts as a Founder in first-person POV.

They create their Founder, choose a career, load into Origin Planet and guide a primitive sci-fi colony.

The world starts small:

- campfire
- low food
- no shelter
- storm coming
- six AI citizens

The world grows into:

- settlement
- town
- industrial colony
- digital city
- futuristic city
- flying vehicles
- spaceport
- portal/world travel

## Gameplay Pillars

### 1. First-Person Founder Freedom

The player can walk around, inspect citizens, interact with objects, give tools, trigger world events and influence decisions.

### 2. AI Citizens With Lives

Agents have roles, goals, moods, memories, relationships and ambitions.

They should act without being fully controlled by the player.

### 3. Creative Mission Structure

Missions are generated from the world's problems.

Examples:

- build shelter before the storm
- secure food supply
- calm a disagreement
- discover a resource deposit
- unlock better tools
- hold the first council

### 4. Evolving Technology

Technology unlocks because society needs solutions.

Problem creates research. Research unlocks technology. Technology unlocks buildings. Buildings unlock jobs.

### 5. Vehicles and Travel Later

Vehicles should come from tech progression:

- carts/transport tools
- basic ground vehicles
- electric vehicles
- drones
- flying cars
- spacecraft

### 6. World Scale Later

Start with one settlement.

Grow to districts.

Then cities.

Then spaceport.

Then multiple worlds.

## UI Format

Use a clean sci-fi HUD:

- top resource bar
- world status panel
- selected citizen panel
- mission tracker
- activity feed
- map/compass later
- tech tree strip
- world event controls
- career/profile panel

Do not overload the first screen.


Core experience:

```text
Create Founder
```

## Why This Can Become Better

Traditional open worlds often rely heavily on scripted NPC behaviour.

NEXUS Worlds should aim for:

- citizens who remember
- missions that emerge from problems
- technology that grows from needs
- worlds that evolve over time
- player-created personal stories

This is the path to making the game feel more alive than a standard open-world sandbox.

## Bottom Line

NEXUS Worlds should use GTA-style open-world freedom and Star Citizen-style sci-fi scale as structural inspiration, but the actual game should be an original AI civilisation experience where the player's Founder story shapes a living, evolving world.
